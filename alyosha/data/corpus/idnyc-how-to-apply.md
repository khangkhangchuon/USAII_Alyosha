# How to apply for an IDNYC card

IDNYC is the free municipal ID card for New Yorkers. It is useful as a stepping
stone for people who are waiting on a NY State ID, because many city services
and some private businesses accept it.

## Who qualifies

All New Yorkers ages 10 and up qualify. There are **no restrictions based on
immigration status, criminal record, or housing status**.

## Step 1 — figure out which documents you need

Use the IDNYC Document Calculator at
<https://www.nyc.gov/site/idnyc/card/documentation.page> to see which documents
you can bring. Requirements vary based on your situation.

## Step 2 — book an appointment or walk in

Walk-in locations (no appointment needed):

- **Tuesdays:** IDNYC East New York — 2400 Fulton St, 5th floor
- **Wednesdays:** Bronx Monterey HRA Center — 1910 Monterey Ave, 5th floor
- **Thursdays:** IDNYC Union Square — 109 E 16th St, 1st floor
- **Fridays:** Queens Public Library, Jamaica Branch — 89-11 Merrick Blvd,
  1st floor

For all other locations, book online at the IDNYC enrollment center locator.
New appointment slots open every Friday for the following week.

## Step 3 — go to your appointment

Bring your documents to your visit.

## Cost & timeline

The card is **free**. The page does not specify a turnaround time. NYC reports
the IDNYC is free for all New Yorkers who apply through December 31, 2026.

## Help

- **Call 311** to ask questions about IDNYC.
- IDNYC website: <https://www.nyc.gov/site/idnyc/>.
